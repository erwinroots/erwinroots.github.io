Descendants of Samuel Lockwood Close


First Generation

1. Samuel Lockwood Close,1 son of Benjamin Close Jr and Elizabeth Gale, was born on 21 Sep 1827 in Sullivan, Lorain, Ohio,2 died on 29 Nov 1914 in Grants Pass, Josephine, Oregon1 at age 87, and was buried in Grants Pass, Josephine, Oregon (Granite Hill Cemetery, Grants Pass, Oregon).3

General Notes: Samuel usually went by Lockwood. He was a descendant of "Goodman" Close, a farmer in Connecticut, who came to America about 1642.

Research Notes: The family could not be found in the 1870 census.

Noted events in his life were:

1. Occupation: Farmer. 4

2. Marriage License: 9 Aug 1850, Lorain County, Ohio. 5

3. Census: 8 Oct 1850, Sullivan, Ashland, Ohio. 4 Occupation: farmer

4. Census: 29 Jun 1860, Sullivan, Ashland, Ohio. 6 Samuel's mother, Elizabeth (Gale) Close lived next door.

5. Military: Civil War, 7 Oct 1862. 7 Lockwood enlisted in the army, but was discharged after one month and sixteen days.

6. Census: 1 May 1875, Garden City, Blue Earth, Minnesota. 8 Lockwood was the assessor and census taker for Garden City.

7. Census: 8 Jun 1880, Garden City, Blue Earth, Minnesota. 9 Son Leslie and family were visiting.

8. Residence: 1890, Garden City, Blue Earth, Minnesota. 7 Lockwood was enumerated in the 1890 special census of Civil War Union veterans and widows of veterans.

9. Census: 27 Jun 1900, East Grand Forks, Polk, Minnesota. 10 Lockwood, a widower, was living in a house and had a lodger.

10. Census: 3 May 1910, Lake Crystal, Blue Earth, Minnesota. 11 Lockwood was living in a boarding house.

11. Residence: Bef 29 Nov 1914, Grants Pass, Josephine, Oregon. 1 Lockwood moved to Oregon where he lived with his son Perry and family.

Samuel married Mary Louisa Foote,12 daughter of Charles Foote and Marcia Hunter, on 12 Sep 1850 in Wellington, Lorain, Ohio.12 Mary was born on 8 Jun 1832 in Wellington, Lorain, Ohio,12 died on 26 Jan 1893 in Lake Crystal, Blue Earth, Minnesota13 at age 60, and was buried in Garden City, Blue Earth, Minnesota (Garden City Cemetery, Garden City, Minnesota).14

Marriage Notes: Samuel and Mary wed in August but were enumerated in the census at their parents' homes in October and November. Perhaps Samuel hadn't had time to set up his own farm. They might have been living together at one or the other residences, but happened to be at their own homes when the census was taken.

General Notes: Mary is descended from Mayflower passenger William White.

Noted events in her life were:

1. Census: 14 Nov 1850, Wellington, Lorain, Ohio. 15

Second Generation (Children)

2. Leslie Walter Close 12 (Samuel Lockwood1) was born on 5 Oct 1851 in Sullivan, Ashland, Ohio,12 died on 27 Jul 1895 in Little Rock, Pulaski, Arkansas16 at age 43, and was buried in Garden City, Blue Earth, Minnesota.16

General Notes: In 1893 Leslie abandoned his wife and four children. In 1895 he was working as a clerk in Little Rock, so the move wasn't temporary. The cause of his death was listed as "insolation" or heatstroke which may indicate alcoholism, "Exposure to hot, humid temperatures is the main cause of heatstroke, but alcohol can make it more likely because it can affect your body's ability to regulate temperature."

Noted events in his life were:

1. Residence: 1 May 1875, Garden City, Blue Earth, Minnesota. 24 Leslie and Adella were still childless when they moved to Minnesota in company with Lockwood's family.

2. Residence: Bef 1878, Otter Tail County, Minnesota. Before their first child was born, Leslie and Adella established a homestead in Minnesota near the town of Deer Creek. Their first three children were all born here with their births registered in Deer Creek.

3. Census: Jun 1880, Garden City, Blue Earth, Minnesota. Leslie, Adella, daughter Minnie, and son Bennie made a trip back to Minnesota to visit Leslie parents.

4. Residence: 1 May 1885, Henning, Otter Tail, Minnesota. 25 Leslie moved from the homestead to the town of Henning where he worked in a store. Perhaps he sold the homestead after gaining ownership of it. In Henning he worked in a store.

5. Land: 4 May 1885, Otter Tail County, Minnesota. 26 In accordance with the Homestead Act of 1862, Leslie was granted ownership of his 167 acre homestead. The family had to have occupied the land for five years before receiving the grant.

6. Residence: Abt 1886, Deer Creek, Otter Tail, Minnesota. 27 Leslie, "had charge of building of the big [grain] elevator there." Daughter Sadie was born while the family was living here.

7. Residence: Abt 1888, Henning, Otter Tail, Minnesota. 27 The family moved back to Henning where Leslie was a grain buyer. Daughter Minnie died here.

8. Residence: Abt May 1890, Deer Creek, Otter Tail, Minnesota. 27 The family once again moved back to Deer Creek.

9. Residence: 1893, Little Rock, Pulaski, Arkansas. 27

Leslie married Pamela Adella Stevens,28 daughter of Samuel Orange Stevens Jr and Marinda Mary Burge, on 29 Oct 1873 in Rochester, Lorain, Ohio.29 Pamela was born on 1 Dec 1852 in Knox County, Illinois28 and died on 26 May 1904 in Mankato, Blue Earth, Minnesota30 at age 51.
